Oliver Twist, povertà e Intelligenza Artificiale

Inglese: un'analisi tra Ottocento e presente

An analysis of Dickens's Oliver Twist, combined with a study of work, poverty, and Artificial Intelligence, reveals surprising parallels between Victorian London and our own era.

Oliver, a child exploited in workhouses and forced to steal by Fagin, embodies stark and visible poverty. Today, that poverty has transformed into a digital divide: those without access to the internet or digital skills are excluded from society.

Regarding labor, the physical exploitation denounced by Dickens finds its modern equivalent in the gig economy, where an algorithm hires, evaluates, and fires workers without appeal.

In this sense, Oliver Twist is not just a historical novel, but a lens through which to examine whether progress, without ethics, is simply changing the face of exploitation.
